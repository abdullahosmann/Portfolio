Analog
Reformer Pilates.

A two-week content calendar for a new reformer pilates studio — voice, tone, and visual direction built around quiet physical detail.

(01) The brief

The details are the brand.

Analog isn't a gym. It's a reformer pilates studio — which meant the content couldn't sound like a gym. No hype, no hashtags, no "let's crush it." The brand lives in a quieter register: the sound of vinyl, the warmth of wood, the resistance of steel springs, the quiet before movement begins.

The brief from HG: build a two-week social calendar that would launch the studio with the right voice — educational enough to convert skeptics, atmospheric enough to attract the right crowd.

(02) The system

Posts, stories, reels — one voice.

I built the calendar around three content pillars: the practice (how reformer pilates works, 30-second stretches, breath techniques), the space (the studio, the materials, the atmosphere), and the people (the instructors, the community forming around the brand).

Every post had a TOV line, a caption, and a visual direction — and every one of them pulled from the same tonal palette. "One month of analog. 600+ breaths at a time." "Breath is where the practice begins. Inhale to create space, exhale to find control."

The system meant the studio's social presence sounded consistent from day one — whether it was a carousel about body mechanics or a reel of morning light hitting the reformer.

Analog is built on details. The sound of vinyl. The warmth of wood. The resistance of steel springs. — Analog launch copy
