(01) The brief

Let the product do the talking.

Ralph's is a German bakery — which means the food sells itself, if you get out of its way. The brief wasn't to invent a bold positioning or run a stunt. It was to build a monthly content plan that made the brand feel consistent, warm, and confidently itself across 30 days.

Bright mornings. Gentle nights. One place that feels right at every hour. That became the throughline for January — a month that uses time of day as a structural device, showing the bakery's range from 11 AM breakfasts to 8 PM dinners.

(02) The plan

Feed, stories, reels — every week.

The calendar covered the full January month with a balanced mix of formats: hero product shots, atmospheric reels about the space, behind-the-scenes stories, and community-first posts that celebrated regulars.

Each piece came with a brief, TOV, idea, and caption — so the creative team could execute without having to re-invent the voice every time they sat down to produce.
